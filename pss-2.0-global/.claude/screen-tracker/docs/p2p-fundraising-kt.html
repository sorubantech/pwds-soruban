Peer-to-Peer Fundraising

Your supporters raise money for you. The NGO runs a parent campaign; each supporter spins up their own personal fundraiser page under it and rallies their own network. Every dollar — whether given on the parent page or a supporter's page — rolls up to the campaign total.

▸ Overview

Peer-to-peer (a.k.a. "social" or "supporter") fundraising flips the usual model: instead of the NGO asking donors directly, supporters ask on the NGO's behalf.

The two layers

The parent campaign (e.g. "Run for Clean Water") is the umbrella — one goal, one story, one public landing page. It wraps an existing app.Campaigns record and adds the P2P settings on top.

The fundraisers are the supporters. Each one gets their own child page with a personal story, a personal goal and their own donate button. Sarah running a marathon, a company team, a birthday fundraiser — all live under the same campaign.

Where the money flows

Every gift is one fund.GlobalDonation row. Two nullable FKs tag where it came from:

  • Given on the parent page → P2PCampaignPageId set, P2PFundraiserId NULL
  • Given on a supporter page → both set, so it counts for the supporter and the campaign
  • A CHECK constraint guarantees one page-source per donation
Three screens, one feature. #170 built the engine — the entities, the public pages, the wizard. #15 and #135 are the back-office lenses on top of it: #15 manages campaigns, #135 manages fundraisers. Nothing in #15 or #135 creates new entities — they read and steer what #170 already ships.

1 The Flow — Start to End

The whole P2P journey, in order. The admin builds and publishes the campaign; supporters sign themselves up; donations roll in; the campaign closes and everything totals up.

  1. Create the campaign. Draft In #170's setup editor, fill the 5 tabs — pick the campaign, set fundraiser rules & goal bounds, donation settings, branding, and the email/WhatsApp triggers. Saving wraps an app.Campaigns row and creates the P2P page as Draft.
  2. Publish. Published Validation gates the button (name, slug, type, goal, purpose, story, payment methods). Publishing flips Draft → Published, stamps PublishedAt, and the public URL /p2p/{slug} goes live.
  3. A supporter starts a fundraiser. A visitor clicks "Become a Fundraiser" and walks the 4-step wizard — identity, page setup (title, goal, story, slug), donation settings, then review & activate.
  4. Approval. Active / Pending If the campaign is AUTO, the fundraiser goes live instantly. If MANUAL, it lands as Pending in the approval queue; an admin Approves (→ Active) or Rejects with a reason (→ Rejected) in #135.
  5. Share & donate. The supporter shares their page to their network. Donors give on the child page (counts for the supporter and the campaign) or on the parent page (campaign-direct). Each gift is a GlobalDonation tagged with the right FKs.
  6. Track & steer. Totals, donor counts and the leaderboard update live. Admins watch #15 (campaign roll-up) and #135 (per-fundraiser), pause/resume pages, feature top fundraisers, and message supporters.
  7. Close. Completed At the end date (or "Close Early"), the campaign goes Closed — every child fundraiser auto-flips to Completed, pages turn read-only with a "campaign ended" banner, and a summary email goes out.
In plain words: the admin sets the stage once, then the supporters do the fundraising. The only ongoing admin job is approving newcomers (in MANUAL mode) and keeping an eye on the two grids. When the campaign closes, all the supporter pages freeze and the money is already totalled — because every donation was tagged to its page the moment it came in.

Start-a-fundraiser wizard · /p2p/{slug}/start — 4 steps

  1. Campaign & Identity. Confirm the campaign, link to / create a Contact, enter name & email, pick Individual or Team.
  2. Page Setup. Page title, personal goal (bounded by the campaign), custom URL slug (live availability check), personal story, cover image / video, milestones.
  3. Donation Settings. Use campaign defaults or custom amount chips, employer matching, offline donations, thank-you message, social share copy.
  4. Review & Activate. Summary + preview, then Activate Now / Save as Draft / Schedule — and notification opt-ins. Success screen shows the live URL (or "under review" in MANUAL mode).

Where the money attaches. The existing fund.GlobalDonations table gains two nullable FKs — P2PCampaignPageId and P2PFundraiserId — plus a CHECK constraint NUM_NONNULLS(OnlineDonationPageId, P2PCampaignPageId) <= 1 (one page-source per gift). Totals are pure aggregates:
Roll-upQuery
Fundraiser totalSUM(NetAmount) WHERE P2PFundraiserId = Y
Campaign totalSUM(NetAmount) WHERE P2PCampaignPageId = X (parent-direct + all children)
Team totalSUM(NetAmount) WHERE P2PFundraiserId IN (… TeamId = Z)

5 Statuses & Lookups

P2P statuses are stored as string enums on the entities (not MasterData). The two lifecycles — campaign and fundraiser — run in parallel; closing the campaign cascades its fundraisers.

Campaign page lifecycle

StatusMeaning
DraftBeing built; not public (404 unless preview token)
PublishedLive publicly; accepts registrations & donations
ActiveAuto at start date; same as Published for a timed run
ClosedEnd date or "Close Early" — children → Completed, pages read-only
ArchivedSoft-deleted; public route returns 410 Gone

Fundraiser lifecycle

StatusMeaning
DraftSaved from the wizard, not yet submitted/visible
PendingMANUAL mode — waiting in the approval queue
ActiveLive & taking donations (AUTO, or after approval)
PausedAdmin paused — visible but donations disabled
RejectedAdmin rejected with a reason — not public
CompletedAuto when the parent campaign closes — read-only

Other enumerations

LookupValues
FundraiserApprovalModeAUTO · MANUAL
FundraiserTypeINDIVIDUAL · TEAM
Campaign typeTimed (auto-closes at end date) · Occasion (manual close) · Always-Active (evergreen)
Payment methods (EnabledPaymentMethodsJson)CARD · PAYPAL · APPLEPAY · BANKTRANSFER
Page theme / header styledefault / dark / colorful / minimal · full-width-hero / split / minimal
Achievement badgesFirst Donation · 50% Goal · Goal Reached · Top Fundraiser
Two systems, by design. The campaign and the wrapped app.Campaigns keep separate status — PageStatus (P2P lifecycle) vs Campaign.CampaignStatusId. Don't conflate them: the P2P page can be Draft while its Campaign already exists.
