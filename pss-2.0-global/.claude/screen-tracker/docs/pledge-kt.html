Pledges

A pledge is a donor's promise to give a total over time — "$1,200 across the year, $100 a month". This screen turns that promise into a tracked payment schedule: it auto-builds the installments, watches each one come due, lets staff record the incoming payment against the donation flow, and rolls the whole thing up into On Track / Behind / Overdue / Fulfilled. It is the operations hub for every multi-payment commitment.

▸ Overview

A pledge is a promise, not a transaction. The donor commits a total; the system plans how it will arrive and chases each installment until the whole amount is collected. Unlike a recurring auto-debit, nothing is charged automatically — staff record each payment as it actually lands.

One screen, two entities

Pledge is the parent commitment — donor, total, frequency, dates. PledgePayment is one auto-generated installment row; a pledge owns many. Create a pledge and the child rows are built for you; edit it and the unpaid rows regenerate while paid rows are preserved as history.

Both live under schema fund, under one menu. The screen consumes existing entities — GlobalDonation, Contact, Campaign, DonationPurpose — as FK sources; it doesn't recreate them.

The schedule math

From a total + frequency, the installments fall out:

  • InstallmentAmount = Total ÷ NumberOfInstallments — the final row absorbs any rounding remainder
  • DueDate = StartDate + freqDays × (i − 1) — Monthly = 30, Quarterly = 90, Annual = 365
  • fulfilledAmount = Σ PaidAmount on rows marked Paid
  • outstandingBalance = Total − fulfilledAmount · fulfillment% = fulfilled ÷ Total (capped 100)
Why it matters. Big gifts rarely arrive in one cheque — a $1,200 supporter pays $100 a month. Without a pledge the team has no way to know whether that donor is on schedule, behind, or has quietly stopped. The pledge screen makes the promise visible and chase-able: it knows exactly what's due, what's late, and how much is still outstanding across every multi-payment commitment.

1 The Flow — Start to End

The whole pledge journey, in order. The donor commits a total, the schedule is built, installments come due, staff record each payment against the donation flow, and the pledge climbs toward fulfilled — or gets flagged behind / overdue along the way.

  1. Donor commits a total. A supporter promises, say, $1,200 over the year. An admin opens the Pledge form, picks the donor, purpose (and optional campaign), enters the total, currency and start date.
  2. Pick a frequency. Choose Monthly (or Quarterly / Semi-Annual / Annual / Custom). Touch any one of EndDate, installment amount, or count and the others auto-derive — here $1,200 ÷ 12 = $100/mo. A live preview shows the installment dots before saving.
  3. Schedule auto-generated. Scheduled On save, 12 PledgePayment rows are created — installment 1..12, due monthly, $100 each (the last row absorbs any rounding). PledgeCode like PLG-0007 is assigned.
  4. An installment comes due. Upcoming As each due date arrives the row is Upcoming; if its date passes unpaid, a lazy flip on read marks it Overdue and it shows in the overdue banner.
  5. Record the payment. Paid "Record Payment" deep-links to the donation form, donor & purpose pre-filled. Saving creates a GlobalDonation that links back — stamping PaidDate, PaidAmount and flipping the installment Paid.
  6. Pledge tracks its health. On Track fulfilledAmount climbs with each paid row. The pledge status is computed on every read: paying on schedule → On Track; falling behind the expected-by-today line → Behind; a late row → Overdue.
  7. Fulfilled — or cancelled. Fulfilled When fulfilledAmount ≥ Total the pledge is Fulfilled (terminal). A pledge can instead be Cancelled with a reason — paid history is kept, remaining unpaid rows are dropped, no further edits allowed.
In plain words: the form turns one promise into a row of dated installments; from then on the team's job is to record each payment as it lands and watch the fulfilment bar fill. The only money that actually moves is the donor's own payment at each "Record" — everything else is scheduling, chasing and roll-up so nothing slips through unnoticed.

The schedule fields auto-calc with touched-field tracking: touch EndDate → derive count + amount; touch amount → derive count + EndDate; touch count → derive amount + EndDate. Custom frequency turns auto-calc off — you enter count and amounts by hand. Validation enforces |Total − (amount × count)| ≤ $1.00.

5 Statuses & Lookups

A pledge carries a computed status (five MasterData rows, typeCode PLEDGESTATUS); each installment carries its own (four rows, PLEDGEPAYMENTSTATUS). The pledge status is re-derived on every read — only Cancelled is persisted.

Pledge lifecycle (computed)

StatusMeaning
On TrackDefault — fulfilling per schedule (no late rows, not behind)
Behindfulfilled < expected-by-today (installment × periods elapsed)
OverdueAt least one installment is past due & unpaid
Fulfilledfulfilled ≥ Total — terminal
CancelledManually cancelled with reason — terminal, persisted

Payment status (per installment)

StatusMeaning
PaidA donation is linked; PaidDate / PaidAmount stamped
UpcomingDue date is at or near today, not yet paid
ScheduledDefault — due date is in the future
OverdueDue date passed, still unpaid (lazy-flipped on read)

Each GetAll runs an UPDATE flipping past-due Upcoming rows → Overdue before the SELECT.

How the pledge status is computed (in order)

CheckResult
CancelledAt IS NOT NULL→ Cancelled (terminal, persisted)
fulfilledAmount ≥ TotalPledgedAmount→ Fulfilled
any installment is Overdue→ Overdue
fulfilled < InstallmentAmount × floor((today − Start) ÷ freqDays)→ Behind
otherwise→ On Track

The schedule math, in detail

MechanicHow it works
InstallmentAmountTotal ÷ NumberOfInstallments; the final row absorbs the rounding remainder so installments sum exactly to the total
DueDateStartDate + freqDays × (i − 1) — Monthly 30 · Quarterly 90 · Semi-Annual 180 · Annual 365
Installment-sum ruleValidation requires |Total − (amount × count)| ≤ $1.00
OverpaymentIf PaidAmount > DueAmount, fulfillment% caps at 100 and outstandingBalance at 0
CancelKeeps paid history, drops unpaid rows, blocks further edits — cannot cancel when Fulfilled or already Cancelled

LookupValues
Frequency (MasterData PLEDGEFREQUENCY)MONTHLY (30) · QUARTERLY (90) · SEMIANNUAL (180) · ANNUAL (365) · CUSTOM (0) (dataValue = day count)
Pledge status (PLEDGESTATUS)ONTRACK · FULFILLED · OVERDUE · BEHIND · CANCELLED (UPPERCASE; ONTRACK default)

MenuPLEDGE under CRM_DONATION → crm/donation/pledge (OrderBy 5)
Status is FK MasterData, not a string enum. The pledge & payment statuses are real MasterData FKs, seeded UPPERCASE (ONTRACK, PAID, …). Code that compares against PascalCase ("OnTrack", "Paid") silently misses — match on the code (or the resolved name), the same trap noted across the case-management and matching-gift screens. The frequency's dataValue is the day count ("30", "90"), used both for schedule math and the FE suffix map ("/mo", "/qtr").
