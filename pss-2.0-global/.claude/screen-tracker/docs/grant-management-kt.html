Grant Management — Knowledge Transfer

How a grant travels from a funding prospect, through application and approval, into an active funded project with reports — explained simply so a new team member can start testing on day one.

Module: CRM · Grants Screen #62 · FLOW 7-stage pipeline + Funder Reports #63

What is it? Grant Management tracks money the NGO applies for from big institutional funders (foundations, governments, multilaterals). A Grants officer logs a prospect, drafts and submits an application, waits for the funder's decision, and once approved runs the funded project — receiving money in tranches, spending against a budget, and sending reports back to the funder until the grant is closed. Each Grant links a Funder (an organisation contact), a managing Branch, a Currency, and an assigned grant manager.

This guide has only four parts: ① the flow, ② a live animated example, ③ the test cases, and ④ the data tables. Nothing else to memorise.

1 The Flow — Start to End

A grant moves through 7 main stages (shown as a kanban pipeline on screen). Three "off-ramp" stages handle the bad endings.

The seven main stages

StageMeaningWhat unlocks the next move
ProspectA funding opportunity we're eyeing. Nothing submitted yet.Draft the application
ApplicationWe've submitted our proposal to the funder.Requires an Executive Summary; stamps the Submitted date
Under ReviewFunder acknowledged receipt and is evaluating.Funder gives a decision
ApprovedWe won it! Award amount confirmed.Requires Awarded Amount; stamps Decision date
ActiveProject is running. Money flows in, expenses go out.Requires Funder Grant Number + Start/End dates
ReportingIn the reporting window — reports due to the funder.All deliverables + reports done
ClosedFinished and signed off. Read-only.Terminal
⚠ The three off-ramps. A grant can also end early: Rejected (funder said no — needs a Rejection Reason), On Hold (paused while Active), and Cancelled (we withdrew before it went Active). These are real saved stages, not calculated.

The journey in plain words

  1. Log the prospect. Officer clicks + New Grant Application, fills funder + grant details + budget lines + milestones, and Saves as Draft. The system auto-stamps a code (GRT-012) and the grant sits in Prospect.
  2. Submit. Officer clicks Submit Application → stage moves to Application, the submitted date is stamped, and the proposal goes to the funder.
  3. Wait for review. Once the funder acknowledges, stage moves to Under Review.
  4. Decision. If we win, Approve records the Awarded Amount and moves it to Approved. If we lose, Reject with a reason ends it at Rejected.
  5. Activate. Activate Grant records the funder's grant number + project dates → stage Active. Now money can be received as tranches and expenses logged against the budget.
  6. Report. During the grant, the officer submits Funder Reports (see the report cycle below). Stage shows Reporting when a report window is open.
  7. Close. Close Grant wraps it up → Closed and read-only.

The Funder Report cycle (screen #63)

Once a grant is Active, each report the officer sends the funder runs its own little loop:

stateDiagram-v2
    direction LR
    [*] --> Draft: create report
    Draft --> Submitted: Submit to Funder
    Submitted --> Accepted: funder accepts
    Submitted --> RevisionRequested: funder asks for changes
    RevisionRequested --> Draft: reopen & fix
    Accepted --> [*]
In plain words: a report starts as Draft, gets Submitted to the funder, and either comes back Accepted (done) or with a Revision Requested — which drops it back to Draft so the officer can fix and resubmit. Submitting on time is what feeds the grant's Compliance Rate.
