Executive Summary

The Q4 2025 financial report covers expenditures from October through December 2025. During this period, the Clean Water Initiative continued to make strong progress toward its targets. Total expenditures for the quarter were $83,000, bringing cumulative spending to $325,000 (65% of the total $500,000 budget). All spending categories remain within their allocated budgets with no significant variances to report.

Deliverable Progress

32 of 50 wells have been constructed (64%). 28 of 50 target communities are now being served (56%). Water quality testing is on track at 145 of 200 planned tests (73%). 52 of 100 training sessions have been completed (52%).

Challenges & Risks

Supply chain delays in the East Africa region caused a two-week setback in Q4 construction timelines. The team has secured alternative suppliers and expects to recover lost time in Q1 2026. Currency fluctuations in local markets created a 3% variance in travel costs, which was absorbed within the existing contingency allocation.

Impact Stories

In the Makueni community, the newly constructed well now serves over 1,200 residents who previously walked 4 km to access clean water. Community leader Mary Mwikali shared: "Our children no longer miss school because of waterborne illnesses. This well has transformed our village." The community has formed a water management committee of 12 members who maintain the well and collect modest user fees to ensure long-term sustainability.
