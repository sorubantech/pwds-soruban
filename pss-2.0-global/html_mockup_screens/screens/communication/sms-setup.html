Country-Specific Compliance Notes

🇮🇳 India - TRAI DLT Regulations
Key Requirements:
  • All SMS senders must register on the DLT (Distributed Ledger Technology) platform
  • Entity ID, Sender ID (Header), and Content Templates must be pre-registered
  • Promotional SMS only allowed between 9:00 AM - 9:00 PM IST
  • Transactional SMS allowed 24/7 but must match registered templates
  • Scrubbing against NCPR (National Customer Preference Register) is mandatory
  • Content must include entity name and opt-out instructions
Your DLT registration is active
🇺🇸 United States - TCPA & 10DLC
Key Requirements:
  • TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act) requires prior express written consent for marketing messages
  • 10DLC (10-Digit Long Code) registration required for A2P messaging
  • Brand and campaign must be registered with The Campaign Registry (TCR)
  • Must include opt-out instructions (e.g., "Reply STOP to unsubscribe")
  • Cannot send messages before 8:00 AM or after 9:00 PM recipient's local time
  • Violations can result in fines of $500 - $1,500 per message
10DLC campaign approved
🇬🇧 United Kingdom - ICO / PECR
Key Requirements:
  • PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations) governs SMS marketing
  • Consent must be specific, informed, and freely given (GDPR-aligned)
  • Must identify the sender in every message
  • Charities have a "soft opt-in" exception for existing donors
  • Must provide a simple opt-out mechanism
  • ICO can fine up to 500,000 GBP for violations
Soft opt-in enabled for donors
🇦🇪 UAE - TRA Regulations
Key Requirements:
  • TRA (Telecommunications Regulatory Authority) approval required for all bulk SMS
  • Sender ID must be registered and approved by TRA
  • Content must be reviewed and approved before sending
  • Arabic language option must be provided where applicable
  • Promotional SMS restricted during Ramadan and public holidays
  • All messages must comply with UAE content laws
Sender ID pending TRA review

How SMS Segments Work: A single SMS can contain up to 160 characters using GSM-7 encoding (standard Latin characters). Messages with Unicode characters (emojis, non-Latin scripts) are limited to 70 characters per segment.
